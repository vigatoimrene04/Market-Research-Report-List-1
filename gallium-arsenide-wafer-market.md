<p><p>Gallium Arsenide (GaAs) wafers are primarily categorized into two types: Semi-Insulating (SI) GaAs and Semi-Conducting (SC) GaAs. SI GaAs wafers are used for high-frequency and high-power applications due to their ability to isolate electrical signals, minimizing noise and interference. In contrast, SC GaAs wafers are deployed in electronic and optoelectronic devices, providing effective charge carrier mobility for faster electronic performance. Both types are crucial in the semiconductor industry, particularly in telecommunications and advanced electronics.</p></p>
