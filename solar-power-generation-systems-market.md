<p><p>Solar power generation systems can be categorized into centralized and decentralized photovoltaic (PV) power plants. Centralized PV power plants are large-scale installations that generate electricity from solar energy and feed it directly into the grid, often located in remote areas to optimize space and sunlight exposure. In contrast, decentralized PV power plants typically consist of smaller installations distributed across various locations, such as residential rooftops or commercial facilities, allowing for localized energy generation and consumption, enhancing energy independence.</p></p>
