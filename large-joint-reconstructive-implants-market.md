Metallic implants, often made of titanium or cobalt-chromium alloys, offer strength and durability. Ceramic implants provide a smoother surface and better wear resistance, reducing friction and extending implant lifespan. Polymers, typically used as bearing surfaces or in conjunction with other materials, are lightweight and flexible, enhancing comfort. Each type presents unique advantages tailored to patient needs and surgical outcomes.</p></p>
